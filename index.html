MICHAEL DAVIES is a BAFTA and RTS award-winning writer and director. Following a BA in Drama from Manchester University and an MA in Film and Television from Bristol University, he began his career at BBC Television Features, working on a wide range of projects, including his critically-acclaimed documentary series Barnardo’s Children.

Michael has made observational documentaries, personal testimony based documentaries, drama-documentaries, dramatised adventure, comedy and factual entertainment programmes as well as writing and directing drama.

His first short film What’s ‘Virgin’ Mean? has screened at over fifty international festivals, winning seven awards and attracting a total of more than nine million hits on YouTube. Unusually for a short film, it also made a profit.

Michael was selected for the BFI's Think Shoot Distribute course for ‘emerging feature film directors’, held at the London Film Festival, and won a place on the Directing Actors course at the NFTS. He was offered the chance to direct the re-boot of the hit BBC children’s drama Tracy Beaker Returns, and worked on all three series, winning a BAFTA and two RTS awards.

His second short, Love At First Sight, starring Phyllida Law and John Hurt, was funded by the Wellcome Trust and a Secret Millionaire (see below). It won numerous awards, including an RTS, Rushes and Raindance, and was shortlisted for an Oscar.

Michael’s successful association with the Wellcome Trust led to a commission to write his first feature film script, Psychopomp.
